See www.ukca.ac.uk Called from ASAD_CDRIVE!> Method !> Bimolecular rate coefficients are calculated from the Ahrenius expression: k(T) = k * (T/300)^a * exp(-b/T) where T is the temperature (in K). The parameters k, a and b are taken from the bimolecular ratefile ratb.d. The reactions CO + OH -> H + CO2 and OH + HONO2 are pressure or density dependent and therefore need to be calculated separately. However, this code should never need changing even if neither of these reaction are included in the chemistry. The reactions HO2+MeCO3, MeOO+MeOO and OH+C3H8 have temperature dependent branching ratios. Therefore, they are calculated separately.
