13 ! MMethod:
14 ! Calculation of the PM10 and PM2.5 mass concentrations (in
15 ! microgram/m3) by using the cumulative volume (from 0 to
16 ! cut-off diameter) of a log-normal, with median radius
17 ! (or gemetric mean radius) r_bar, geometric standard deviation
18 ! sigma and total number Ntot.
19 ! The contributions by the different aerosol species and modes
20 ! are added up to get the total PM10 & PM2.5 concs (microgram/m3).
21 !
22 ! Formulation derived from eqs. (7.51) & (7.51a) of Seinfeld
23 ! and Pandis (1998) using method of (7.39). Also, checked with
24 ! "The Mechanics of Inhaled Pharmaceutical Aerosols: An
25 ! Introduction", by Warren A. Finlay, Academic Press, 2001
26 !
27 ! (1) Cumulative_volume for particles with diameter between
28 ! 0 and d_cut (assuming spherical particles):
29 ! V(d_cut) = (A*Ntot/2.0) + (A*Ntot/2.0) *
30 ! erf((alog(d_cut)-B)/(sqrt(2.0)*alog(sigma)))
31 ! with
32 ! A = (pi/6.0)*exp(3.0*alog(dbar)+4.5*(alog(sigma))**2)
33 ! B = alog (volume median diameter) =
34 ! = alog(d_bar)+3.0*(alog(sigma))**2
