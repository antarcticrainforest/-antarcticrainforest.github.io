245 ! The following formula is used to calculate the effective Henry's Law coef,
246 ! which takes the effects of dissociation and complex formation on a species'
247 ! solubility (see Giannakopoulos, 1998)
248 !
249 ! H(eff) = K(298)exp{[-deltaH/R]x[(1/T)-(1/298)]}
250 !
251 ! The data in columns 1 and 2 above give the data for this gas-aqueous transfer,
252 ! Column 1 = K(298) [M/atm]
253 ! Column 2 = -deltaH/R [K-1]
254 !
255 ! If the species dissociates in the aqueous phase the above term is multiplied by
256 ! another factor of 1+{K(aq)/[H+]}, where
257 ! K(aq) = K(298)exp{[-deltaH/R]x[(1/T)-(1/298)]}
258 ! The data in columns 3 and 4 give the data for this aqueous-phase dissociation,

264 ! Column 5 = K(298) [M]
